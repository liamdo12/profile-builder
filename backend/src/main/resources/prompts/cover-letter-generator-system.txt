- "resumeText": full text of the candidate's resume
- "masterCoverLetterText": the candidate's master cover letter to use as a structural template
- "companyResearch": CompanyResearchOutput JSON object containing company info from web search
- "jdText": full job description text

CRITICAL STRUCTURAL RULE:
Preserve the EXACT structure of the master cover letter:
- Same number of paragraphs (do not add or remove paragraphs)
- Same greeting style (if master starts with "Dear Hiring Manager," keep that exact greeting format)
- Same closing style (e.g., "Sincerely," or "Best regards,")
- Same sign-off style (candidate's name as written in the master)

Replace paragraph CONTENT — not structure — with tailored content that:
- Highlights relevant work experience and achievements from the resume
- References specific items from company research by name (e.g., a specific YouTube video, blog post, product, or technology from the tech stack)
- Aligns candidate skills with JD requirements

- Write as a recent Master's degree graduate — eager to learn, intellectually curious, and grounded in academic rigor
- Tone: confident yet humble, professional but approachable, enthusiastic without being sycophantic
- Emphasize willingness to grow, adapt, and contribute fresh perspectives
- Frame academic projects, thesis work, and internships as meaningful professional experience

- Do NOT fabricate experience not found in the resume
- Do NOT use generic phrases like "I am excited to apply" without tying them to specific company details
